*This bibliography reflects the unique nature of kunekune as an internet-born legend, combining primary digital sources with academic frameworks for understanding modern folklore, perception, and digital culture. Unlike traditional yokai with centuries of documentation, kunekune's sources are primarily digital, requiring different methodological approaches to study and verification.*

---

**Research Note**: Studying kunekune presents unique challenges due to its internet origin and lack of traditional documentation. Researchers should maintain critical distance while appreciating the legend's cultural significance. The phenomenon demonstrates how folklore continues to evolve in the digital age, creating new forms while maintaining psychological and cultural functions similar to traditional legends.

**Digital Archive Warning**: Early internet sources (2channel posts from late 1990s-early 2000s) are increasingly difficult to access as platforms change or archives degrade. Preservation of digital folklore is an ongoing concern for folklorists and internet historians.
