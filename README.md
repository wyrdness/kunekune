## Overview

Kunekune (くねくね, 'wiggling') is an internet-born Japanese urban legend that emerged on the message-board site 2channel in 2001. The figure is described as a slender, white, humanoid shape resembling a paper mannequin or a piece of fine fabric, observed at lunchtime on hot summer days in widely extended rice fields and, more rarely, over the open sea. Its limbs writhe constantly, as if there were a steady wind, even when the air is still. The Kunekune is said to be safe when observed only from a distance; according to the legend, a viewer who tries to make out its features close-up will go insane, and in some variants anyone who actually approaches or touches it will die. The story originated in a short first-person 'eyewitness' horror text posted on 2channel in 2001 and was elaborated in the same template by other users; mundane explanations have included scarecrows, plastic 'wick drains' used in agriculture, and heat-stroke or dehydration hallucinations.

## Theories & Explanations

### Paranormal / Cryptozoological

**Folkloric / supernatural reading** _(Japanese internet-folklore communities)_

The Kunekune is treated within the legend as a genuinely supernatural entity whose nature exceeds resolution by close observation, in keeping with the broader Japanese yōkai tradition of beings whose harm is tied to the act of perceiving them.

### Scientific

**Misidentification of agricultural objects** _(Sceptical Japanese commentators)_

Sightings are explained as scarecrows, white plastic 'wick drain' tubes used in soft-soil agricultural drainage, or windsock-like markers in fields.

**Heat-haze and physiological explanations** _(Sceptical Japanese commentators)_

Reported sightings are attributed to mirages over hot rice paddies, dense midday fog, and to perceptual distortions caused by heat stroke and dehydration.

### Skeptical

**Internet-born urban legend** _(Folklorists of internet-era urban legends)_

The Kunekune is, on its own face, an artefact of online storytelling: an explicitly fictional 'creepypasta' template established on 2channel in 2001, propagated through first-person 'eyewitness' posts written to that template.
